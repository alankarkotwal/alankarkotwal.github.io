Hidden Markov Model Part-of-Speech Tagging


Guide: Prof. Navin Khaneja, EE, IIT Bombay


The aim of the field of Natural Language Processing is to enable text-based communication between humans and computers. In particular, many challenges in the field focus on language understanding that is, enabling computers to derive meaning from human or natural language input, and others involve natural language generation. A basic component of language understanding is extracting the role of words used in the context of a sentence. Parts of speech are a good way to qualify the roles of words in a sentence. Hidden Markov Models (HMMs) are a good solution to this problem because of their ability to model sequential data well. Here are the used Markov chain and example emissions respectively:






Using POS tags from the Penn Treebank and simple sentences from the CoNLL 2000 data, we trained a model for POS tagging. Our training dataset had 8637 sentences, spanning 45 POS and 17260 words.
