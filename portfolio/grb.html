Detection and Localization of Short Gamma-ray Bursts


Guide: Prof. A. R. Rao, Astrophysics, Tata Institute for Fundamental Research


We did a literature survey on the phenomenon of Gamma Ray bursts, including observations, the internal mechanisms, observational techniques and open problems in the field. Among the open problems and new datasets in the field, we tackle detecting short gamma-ray bursts from data acquired by the CZTI Hard X-Ray Imager on board the Astrosat.




Since gamma–ray sources lie at cosmological distances (which is apparent from the fact that their distribution in the sky is very uniform, and measurements indicate thus), it makes sense to use them as distance measures if we are able to discover some quantity derived from the properties of a burst that is independent of distance from observer and a specific burst. This is analogous to the fact that the intrinsic brightness of Type 1a Supernovae is a constant to a factor of 0.8 to 1.2 (after applying the stretching–luminosity correlation), which helps us bound distances to these objects and therefore get an accurate relation between redshift and distance valid over large time–scales, which can then be used to test cosmological theories.

We processed basic data for some gamma-ray bursts from Astrosat CZTI data and obtained light curves and detector-plane histograms for them. Details here.
