Laparoscopy Image Enhancement


Guide: Prof. Suyash Awate, CSE, IIT Bombay


Laparoscopic images in minimally invasive surgery get corrupted by surgical smoke and noise, like in the image below. This degrades the quality of the surgery and the results of further processing for, say, segmentation and tracking. Algorithms for desmoking and denoising laparoscopic images seem to be missing in the medical vision literature.


We formulated the problem of joint desmoking and denoising of laparoscopic images as a Bayesian inference problem. This formulation relies on a novel probabilistic graphical model of images, which includes a Markov Random Field (MRF) formulation for color-contrast. Another MRF models smoothness on the uncorrupted color image as well as the transmission-map image that indicates color attenuation due to smoke.

The MRF for spatial regularity penalizes, in an edge-preserving way, the differences between pixels in a neighborhood. The color-contrast prior is incorporated by learning marginal gamma distributions on color intensities in clean laparoscopy images and penalizing some measure of distance between this learned prior and the estimated image.


The process starts with estimating the ambient corruption color, which is the color of the ambient light scattered by the smoke. This is typically low in saturation, so regions with low variance in color are selected and their color is projected on a sub-space pre-learned from annotated smoky laparoscopy images.


Optimizing using the above Markov Random fields gives reasonably good reconstructions of the underlying clean images. In the three columns, our result is the left-most, and is compared to two state-of-the-art landscape dehazing methods. Our method preserves the natural-ness and improves visual quality of the images best. More details here, implementation here.
