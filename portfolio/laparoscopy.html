Laparoscopy Image Enhancement


Guide: Prof. Suyash Awate, CSE, IIT Bombay


Laparoscopic images in minimally invasive surgery get corrupted by surgical smoke, specularity and noise, like in the image below. This degrades the quality of the surgery and the results of further processing for, say, segmentation and tracking. Algorithms for desmoking, despeckling and denoising laparoscopic images seem to be missing in the medical vision literature.


We formulated the problem of joint desmoking, despeckling and denoising of laparoscopic images as a Bayesian inference problem. This formulation relies on a novel probabilistic graphical model of images, which includes a Markov Random Field (MRF) formulation for color-contrast. Another MRF models smoothness on the uncorrupted color image as well as the hidden transmission-map image that indicates color attenuation due to smoke. The (fixed) areas in the image that have speckles are filled in with a prior that enforces that patches in the image are sparse in some dictionary learned on clean laparoscopy images.

The MRF for spatial regularity penalizes, in an edge-preserving way, the differences between pixels in a neighborhood. The color-contrast prior is incorporated by learning marginal gamma distributions on color intensities in clean laparoscopy images and penalizing some measure of distance between this learned prior and the estimated image. The dictionary MRF prior enforces a penalty on error in dictionary reconstruction of pixel neighborhoods in the image and on the sparsity of such a reconstruction.


The process starts with estimating the ambient corruption color, which is the color of the ambient light scattered by the smoke. This is typically low in saturation, so regions with low variance in color are selected and their color is projected on a sub-space pre-learned from annotated smoky laparoscopy images.


The above optimization is highly non-convex, and has the transmission map as a hidden random variable. We use the expectation maximization algorithm to combat this. The difficult expectation step is handled using either the mode approximation or Variational Bayes. This gives reasonably good reconstructions of the underlying clean images.
