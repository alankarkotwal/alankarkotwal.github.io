Temperature Controller on a CPLD


Guide: Prof. Jayanta Mukherjee, EE, IIT Bombay


We designed a proportional-derivative temperature controller with the heating/cooling element as a Peltier plate, which is a semiconductor heat exchanger, with the PD algorithm running on a complex programmable logic device. Feedback was taken using a LM35 temperature sensor via an 8-bit ADC. The proportional-derivative controller was programmed on the CPLD with fixed-point arithmetic written from scratch.




The above picture shows the entire system. To the left is the Peltier plate mounted on a metal payload, with the LM35 mounted on top of it. In the middle is the control and temperature display circuit, currently controlling the temperature at 4°C. At the right is the IITB Krypton board on which the PD controller is running. We could accurately control temperatures from -5°C to 50°C to an error of half a degree with this setup.
